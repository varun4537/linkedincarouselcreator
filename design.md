# design.md

## Floating Editorial LinkedIn Carousel Design System

**Reference Style:** Modern editorial micro-blog carousel with floating card effect
**Visual Direction:** Minimal, premium, typography-first, slightly futuristic, social-native design system
**Objective:** Create bold, highly readable LinkedIn/Instagram carousel slides with a premium floating-card aesthetic optimized for both **1080×1080** and **1080×1350** formats.

---

# 1. DESIGN PRINCIPLES

### Design Personality

* Minimal
* Premium
* Editorial
* Modern tech aesthetic
* Typography-first
* Calm but high contrast
* Futuristic without feeling gimmicky

### Design Feel

Think:

* Premium startup founder content
* LinkedIn creator carousel
* Apple + editorial poster design
* High-end SaaS presentation aesthetic
* Modern monochrome storytelling

Keywords:

Minimal
Bold typography
Clean hierarchy
Floating card
Editorial spacing
Premium shadows
Soft depth
Modern monochrome

---

# 2. CORE DESIGN DNA

This design combines:

### 1. Editorial Typography

Large, oversized type hierarchy.

### 2. Floating Card System

Slides should feel like they are floating above the canvas.

### 3. Soft Light + Shadow

Subtle depth creates premium perception.

### 4. Gradient Lighting

Soft glow effect inspired by spotlight or glass diffusion.

### 5. Structured Minimalism

Few elements, strong hierarchy.

The visual feeling should be:

“Premium creator content”

instead of

“Canva business template”

---

# 3. CANVAS SYSTEM

## Supported Sizes

### Square

**1080 × 1080 px**

### Portrait

**1080 × 1350 px**

---

## Adaptation Rules

### Square (1080×1080)

Slide occupies:
**92–96% of canvas**

Margin:
40–60px

Floating effect:
moderate

---

### Portrait (1080×1350)

Critical rule:

The content slide becomes visually smaller to create depth.

Slide occupies:
**84–90% of canvas height**

Outer border area:
60–100px

Purpose:
Creates premium gallery-frame feel.

The negative space around the slide allows:

* floating illusion
* shadow visibility
* visual depth
* luxury aesthetic

Think:

A premium card hovering over background.

---

# 4. FLOATING CARD SYSTEM

## Core Effect

The content card is never edge-to-edge.

Instead:

Canvas Background

↓

Negative Space / Border

↓

Floating Slide Card

↓

Soft Shadow

This creates:

3D perception

without looking cheesy.

---

## Card Style

Radius:
18–28px

Card appearance:
Soft matte finish

No glossy effects.

---

## Shadow System

### Primary Shadow

Soft diffuse shadow

Properties:

Blur:
80–120px

Opacity:
10–18%

Offset:
Y-axis 18–36px

Color:
black with transparency

Purpose:
creates floating depth

---

### Secondary Ambient Shadow

Very soft spread shadow

Blur:
120–160px

Opacity:
4–8%

Purpose:
subtle premium softness

Result:

Slide feels elevated.

Never dramatic.

# 6. LIGHTING & SHADOW EFFECT

## Signature Lighting Effect

The standout visual cue.

A soft vertical spotlight gradient should run through the slide.

Behavior:

Subtle glow

Dark → soft gray diffusion → dark

Almost cinematic.

Example placement:

Center-right

or

Left edge

Purpose:
Creates depth and softness.

Opacity:
Very low.

Never obvious.

Think:

foggy spotlight

not gradient design.

---

## Glow Treatment

Subtle blurred radial light.

Opacity:
5–12%

Blur:
extremely soft

Purpose:
premium mood

# 10. BORDER / DEPTH SYSTEM

Especially for 1080×1350.

Layout logic:

Canvas Background

↓

Soft margin

↓

Floating card

↓

Shadow bleed

The visible outer border area should feel intentional.

Never pure empty space.

Can use:

* subtle noise
* matte texture
* soft light gradient

Very subtle.

---

# 11. RESPONSIVE BEHAVIOR

### For 1080×1080

Content scales up.

Less border.

Stronger slide presence.

---

### For 1080×1350

Content scales down slightly.

More breathing room.

More visible shadow.

More premium feel.

---

# 12. LINKEDIN READABILITY RULES

Everything must work on mobile.

Rules:

* Huge headings
* Minimal copy
* Strong contrast
* No dense paragraphs
* Maximum readability in 2 seconds

Every slide should communicate:

one idea only

---

# 13. DO NOTS

Do not:

* use bright colors
* add gradients everywhere
* overcrowd slides
* overuse decoration
* make shadows harsh
* use glassmorphism
* over-round corners
* use stock-template layouts

Avoid:

cheap Canva aesthetic

Avoid:

corporate PowerPoint feel
